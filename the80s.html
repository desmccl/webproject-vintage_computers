Components in the Macintosh released 1984
The Macintosh came with a Motorola 68000 microprocessor running at 6MHz and 128 KB of built-in RAM For storage, the disk-based computer came with a 400KB single sided 3.5 floppy disk
The display was a built in 9in black and white one-bit CRT monitor with a resolution of 512x342 pixels The keyboard came without arrow keys, numeric keypad and function keys to discourage programmers from porting over existing applications
The computer also shipped with system 1 bundled with MAcPaint and MacWrite as well as Microsoft word The mouse had a single button and did not quite change at all in comparison to its predicessor the Lisa mouse which was the among the first commercial mice sold

Apple Computers of the 80s were very large with limited functionality. They did however really usher in the use of computers in the home.
